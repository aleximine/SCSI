DNA

The Refuge Tower was designed as a facsimile of the historical Towers of Bologna. Layers of stockpiled food and supplies, and a towering height as a physical barrier against airborne disease. Beneath the tower was its Reversal program; 850 meters underground, scientists toiled to develop a means to create inorganic life. Technology limitations prevented digital consciousness, and the risk of Gestaltzerfall was too high for brain transplants. The solution was to convert living people to Silicon-based life; creating a resistance to the disease which wiped out most of the world's population. Yet difficulties arose in mainaining physical structure during conversions.

[White Group]

First tests were unsuccessful in preventing a complete dissolution of the mind during treatment. Subjects came out stark-white due to the conversion of their body to a flesh-feeling Silicon structure, not unlike silicone or rubber.

[Black / Core Structure Group]

The Core Structure group of DNA alterations relied on a solid, crystalline structure be affixed to the subject as a conduit. It was a major leap in progress, yet plagued by a glaring problem. Damage to the core would result in rapid destabilization, and the subject would cease to be more than a puddle. A black dye was added to differentiate this group from the other, as the conduit mask blended in fairly well.

[Hybridization]

The Hybridization group of DNA alterations combined the White group and the Black group. Subjects from both prior test groups were fused together, each with DNA re-added from various animals to help maintain the subject's organs and structure. The result was feral animals; violent, deadly. All were sealed in cryogenic storage.

[Ink Ligament]

The Ink Ligament group of DNA alterations were volatile. A third floor was constructed to house the most dangerous three of these subjects, and were to be constantly monitored by the head research technician, Circi Mandor.

[Iodinic]

The Iodinic group of DNA alterations were the final attempt in creating stable organisms. Stable coreless forms had been made from the combination of puddled Coreless, yet they were incabable of maintaining higher cognitive function following prior death. It was proposed by Wilfred Mandor that the new species be merged with subjects with mind intact. A group of four was taken and allowed contact with a Shaded Coreless. It stripped them of their speech, vocal chords heavily altered, as well as destroying the chemical stability due to high iodine content. But it was a major success for protecting the mind across the treatment. Fear, desires, and bodily needs were maintained in this new form. The properties of these subjects were not fully ideal; the heavy presence of Iodine would cause the flesh and bone to rapidly sublimate in the prescense of most light sources. As such, the entire B1 floor of the DNA Research center had its lights cut, permanently.
